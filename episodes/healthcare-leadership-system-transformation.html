Show Notes

Leadership inside complex healthcare systems and how strategic decisions shape the way organizations actually function. MarKeisha Snaith examines where transformation efforts stall, how communication influences behavior, and the signals leaders often overlook when driving change across large healthcare organizations.

Healthcare transformation requires leaders who understand the gap between strategic intent and operational execution. The signals that matter most inside healthcare systems are often the ones that go unnoticed because they live in the space between departments, between roles, and between what gets measured and what actually happens at the point of care.

Topics covered: healthcare leadership and system transformation, operational reality in large health systems, communication as a leadership tool, AI leadership and system-level change, the gap between strategic planning and frontline execution, and the signals that leaders miss when driving organizational change.
