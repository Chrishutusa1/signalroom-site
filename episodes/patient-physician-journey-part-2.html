How AI Redefines the Patient-Physician Journey (Part 2)

with Dr. Barry Chaiken

Part two of the conversation with Dr. Barry Chaiken continues the exploration of AI in clinical care, diving deeper into implementation challenges and the future of patient-physician dynamics.

Show Notes

In this continuation, Dr. Barry Chaiken delves deeper into the structural challenges that complicate clinical AI adoption. The conversation shifts from foundational principles to the technical and organizational barriers that healthcare systems must overcome. Discussion spans data interoperability, decision support architecture, and how healthcare delivery will transform fundamentally as AI becomes embedded in care workflows.

Healthcare data interoperability remains one of the most persistent barriers to effective clinical AI, yet many organizations treat it as a technical problem rather than a strategic priority. When data flows are fragmented across legacy systems, even sophisticated AI models cannot access the information needed to provide reliable clinical guidance.

Decision support systems only work when embedded into workflows that clinicians already trust. Building that trust requires understanding how physicians actually practice, not imposing workflows that assume AI recommendations should drive decisions.

The future of healthcare delivery will be shaped by how well organizations resolve tensions between AI efficiency and clinical nuance. Leaders who treat AI as a tool for augmenting human judgment rather than replacing it will build more resilient, adaptable systems.
